Part 1

Motivation & Research

1. Motivation (The Why)

Our team chose the C1 Go Runners theme because urban running, although popular, often appears boring and difficult to sustain for beginners. Existing running tools mostly focus on cold data monitoring (such as pace and heart rate) while ignoring the "on-site feeling" and "playfulness" of the running process. Meanwhile, amateur running event organizers lack lightweight digital tools to manage runner experience when designing on-site routes and interactive segments. Our goal is to transform ordinary on-site running into an exciting "exploration mission" through wearable devices and LBS (location-based service) technology, thereby enhancing runners' intrinsic motivation and community connection.

2. Research Gap (The Gap)

Academic Research (4 Papers): Researched academic papers on gamification of sports, wearable device feedback, LBS health applications, and sports social psychology.

Commercial Products (4 Products): Analyzed Strava, Nike Run Club, Keep, and Garmin Connect.

✅ 3 Strengths of Existing Solutions

  • Precise personal health data tracking and visualization analysis.
  • Large-scale online asynchronous social community and route sharing mechanism.
  • Structured post-race/post-run achievement badge system.

❌ 3 Gaps in Existing Solutions

  • Lack of real-time on-site interactive fun (such as virtual "ghost runners" or random treasure hunts based on geofencing).
  • Insufficient support for offline event organizers, making it difficult to quickly set up on-site interactive points.
  • Overemphasis on hardcore data, lacking "game-like" guidance for beginner runners.
