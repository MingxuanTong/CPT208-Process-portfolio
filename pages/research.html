Motivation & Research

1. Motivation

Nowadays running widely accessible and beneficial, but lacks sustained motivation and engagement for many users. Traditional running experiences tend to be repetitive, solitary, and goal-oriented (e.g., distance or time), which can lead to boredom and dropout, especially among beginners. Our project aims to reimagine running as a playful, exploratory activity by integrating game-like elements such as missions, checkpoints, and rewards into real-world environments.

This theme is particularly relevant in today’s context, where people increasingly seek meaningful and enjoyable ways to stay active while balancing busy lifestyles. By transforming ordinary running routes into interactive experiences, we aim to enhance motivation, encourage consistency, and foster a sense of achievement. Additionally, incorporating social and location-based features allows users to connect with others and explore their surroundings in new ways.

Overall, RunLink aligns with the core idea of playful experience design by turning a routine physical activity into an engaging, immersive journey that supports both physical health and emotional satisfaction.

2. Research Gap (The Gap)

Academic Research (4 Papers): Explored gamification in physical activity, motivational mechanisms in exercise apps, effectiveness of gamified health interventions, and location-based interactive experiences.

Commercial Products (4 Products): Strava, Nike Run Club, Zombies, and Runkeeper.

✅ 3 Strengths of Existing Solutions

  • Effective use of gamification to increase motivation.
  • Strong social and community engagement.
  • Integration of real-world activity with digital tracking.

❌ 3 Gaps in Existing Solutions

  • Lack of immersive and playful experience.
  • Limited use of location-based exploration.
  • Weak personalization and adaptive interaction.
