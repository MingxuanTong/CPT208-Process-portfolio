Part 5

Evaluation & Reflection

1. Usability Testing

We invited 3 target users (2 runners, 1 running club event organizer) to conduct on-site testing of the system's Alpha version.

✅ Positive Feedback:

Runners love the "vibration + treasure hunt check-in" prompt on the watch end, finding it very safe without needing to take out their phones.

⚠️ Areas for Improvement:

Event organizers feedback that the mobile drag-and-drop virtual geofence setting interface is a bit crowded and prone to accidental touches.

3. Final Reflection

🌍 Social & Ethical Impact

LBS location tracking involves extremely high privacy sensitivity. Our system designs a "sandbox mode" that only shares during event activities, ensuring location data is automatically destroyed after runners leave the park. Additionally, voice and watch vibration instead of screen reading greatly reduce traffic accident risks caused by runner distraction.

🤖 AI Tool Usage

During the ideation phase and code refactoring, we appropriately used large language models to assist in optimizing copy and CSS layout error correction. All insights on user needs, final interaction plan decisions, and field research were independently completed and fully自主 by team members.
